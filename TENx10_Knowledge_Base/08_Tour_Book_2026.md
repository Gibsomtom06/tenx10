# SECTION 4 — PROMOTER STRATEGY

## 4A — Promoter Responsibilities
All on-the-ground execution. DirtySnatcha team handles national; promoters handle local.

### Marketing
- Create FB event page, add DirtySnatcha + supports as hosts
- Upload assets within 24-48 hours
- Post weekly content, put flyer in ALL local promo groups
- Boost ads $150-300 depending on market
- Use countdown graphics in last 10 days

### Ticketing
- Ticket link live on announcement day
- Fair early-bird + GA price points
- Weekly ticket reports to management

### Production
- Full sound/stage/lighting per tech rider
- Sub-heavy tuning (30-60hz emphasis)
- Safe, clean DJ area
- Smoke/haze permission verification
- Visuals tech if LED wall present

### Hospitality
- 1 hotel room, local transportation, rider items
- Framed Queen of England photo (humor clause)

### Support Budget
- Promoter pays ALL support talent (fees, travel, hospitality, promo graphics)
- DirtySnatcha NEVER covers support costs
- Support costs never reduce headline fee

## 4B — DirtySnatcha Team Responsibilities
- National marketing: tour branding, logos, flyers, animated teasers, promo videos, TikTok edits
- Support artist activation: approvals, group promo pushes, lineup release plans, asset distribution
- Content delivery: visual pack, logo folders, tour headers, flyers in all formats, countdown graphics, press copy
- Advancing: full advance sheet, tech rider, hospitality rider, flight/hotel info, visual files
- Performance: professional DJ set, meet & greet, social content

## 4C — Promoter Behavior by Region
- Midwest: Strong support lineups, FB groups + street teams, receptive to hype videos
- East Coast: Want assets EARLY, hands-on marketing, weekly ticket updates
- West Coast: Love visuals, strong Discord + Reddit, expect creative content
- Southern: Influencer groups, heavy TikTok + IG, expect support to drive attendance
- PNW: Deep rave community, promo groups extremely effective, consistent posting cadence

## 4D — Communication Standard
Promoters MUST: reply within 24 hours, provide weekly ticket counts, confirm ad spend, send on-site contact, confirm load-in time, set times 72 hours before, send stage photos, send sound specs, confirm haze/smoke.
DirtySnatcha team MUST: approve all flyers, provide assets quickly, provide travel info early, maintain clear expectations, respond promptly.

# SECTION 6 — NATIONAL MARKETING STRATEGY

## Five-Phase Marketing Calendar

### Phase 1 — Announcement Week (T-12+ weeks)
Assets: Full tour flyer, city versions, name/title animation, 15/30/60 sec hype trailers
Actions: National rollout all socials, asset pack to promoters, website update, email blast, SMS blast, press release, support artist resharing

### Phase 2 — Early-Bird Push (T-11 to T-9)
Goals: Build momentum, fast ticket movement, plant "alien invasion" narrative
Content: Meme posts, transmission videos, fan reactions, countdown "abduction warnings"

### Phase 3 — Mid-Campaign (T-8 to T-4)
Content: Heavy TikTok + Reels, fan UGC reposts, live clips, alien humor skits, support artist push, collaborations
Promoter minimum: 2 posts/week + paid ads active

### Phase 4 — Final 10-Day Push (MOST IMPORTANT for conversions)
Content: Story countdowns, last-call videos, venue shoutouts, high-energy drop reels, support hype

Mandatory: "ALIEN LANDING IN ___" graphics, on-site hype clips, meet & greet announcements, final ticket push morning-of
During/After: Capture content for recap, tag festival/rave pages, build future-city demand

# SECTION 8 — SPONSORSHIP PACKAGE

## Why This Tour Is Sponsorable
1. Unique alien-invasion theme works for brand activations
2. High-engagement TikTok/Instagram/Reels fanbase
3. 20+ city run = repeat exposure across multiple states
4. Built-in content opportunities (transmissions, UFO graphics, invasion messages)
5. Strong brand alignment with rave culture, nightlife, bass music, futuristic youth marketing

## Sponsorship Tiers

### Tier 1 — Supporting Sponsor ($2,000)
Logo on flyers (footer), 1 cross-promotional giveaway, 1 TikTok/IG Reel shoutout, website placement, merch table signage.
Ideal for: Local clothing brands, small vape shops, nightlife companies.

### Tier 2 — Co-Presenting Sponsor ($5,000)
Everything in Tier 1 plus: mid-tier logo placement, sponsor highlight in announcement posts, product sampling at 5 shows, 4 social integrations, backstage signage, co-branded "alien transmission" video.
Ideal for: Regional energy drink brands, ravewear companies, smoke shop chains.

### Tier 3 — Presenting Sponsor ($10,000-$15,000)
"Take Us To Your Dealer Tour — Presented by [Sponsor]". Top placement on ALL flyers, logo on all videos/animations/teasers, 10+ social integrations, product activation at every show, co-branded merch, exclusive meet & greet rights, sponsor-branded visuals during set.
Ideal for: Major vape brands, beverage companies, LED/tech sponsors, national cannabis brands.

# SECTION 9 — TECHNICAL + HOSPITALITY RIDER

## Technical Requirements
- 2x CDJ-3000 (preferred) or CDJ-2000NXS2 fallback
- 1x DJM-900NXS2 or DJM-V10
- Stable DJ table (36-38 inches)
- Stereo XLR outputs to house PA
- 20,000 watts minimum total output
- Strong sub response 30-60 Hz
- FOH engineer required
- Ground-stacked subs preferred
- No hard limiters clipping artist set
- LED wall or projector for visuals (USB visuals package provided)
- Haze/smoke allowed unless restricted

## Hospitality
- 12x bottled water, 6x energy drinks
- Snack assortment (chips, granola bars, fruit)
- 1 clean towel, 1 hot meal or $30 buyout
- Private or semi-private backstage room with seating for 2-3, outlets, mirror

## The Humor Clause (Required)
"One framed photo of the Queen of England performing an activity relevant to the region of the show — examples: eating deep-dish pizza in Chicago, riding a moose in Montana, attending jury duty in Pittsburgh."

## Hotel
- 1 room, non-smoking, 3-star minimum
- Early check-in/late checkout if routing requires

## Transport
- Airport pickup (shuttle, Uber, Lyft, or driver)
- Transport to hotel and venue, parking instructions

## Merch
- 6' or 8' table, tablecloth, 2 chairs, good lighting
- Square payments (artist brings hardware)
- Standard split: 80/20 or 90/10 artist-favoring

## Show Day Run of Show
- Arrival: 3-5 hours before doors
- Load-In: USB check, sound check, visuals loaded
- Hospitality: Upon arrival
- Set Times: Provided 48-72 hours in advance
- Post-Show: Merch settlement, payment, drop-off

## Payment Terms
- Cash, check, Zelle, or wire
- Due night of show before DirtySnatcha's set
- Deposit as specified in contract
